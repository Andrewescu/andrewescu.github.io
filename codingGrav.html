Coding Gravity

Modeling a basic form of gravity is actually a lot easier than you might think. But first we have to have an understanding of what gravity is. Gravity is a force, more specifically it is an accerelarating force. Every second the force of Earth's gravity is accerelarating you at a rate of around 9.8 meters per second. What this essentially means is that every second gravity is applying a force on you such that if you were in free fall your velocity would increase by 9.8 meters per second every second. For example, lets say we had box that we dropped from the air. And lets say this box was not moving at all when we released it, so it's velocity was zero. After one second it would be falling at a speed of 9.8 meters per second. After two seconds it would be at 19.6 meters per second, and so on.
